# Weekly audit — {{date:YYYY-MM-DD}}

90 minutes (45 once it's a habit). Phone in another room. Same time each week.

## 1. Warm-up (10 min)

Quick scan: what was this week actually like? Not a recap of events — a feeling. One paragraph.



## 2. Triage the dragons (10 min)

Open this week's daily notes. Pull every dragon spotted. Sort:

**Repeats worth solving** (will keep biting if ignored) → open or update a [[Dragons/]] note
- 

**One-offs** → drop, don't carry forward
- 

**Too big to attack directly** → break into a smaller piece
- 

## 3. Negotiate one repeat (40 min)

Pick the most painful one from the list above. Answer in writing, honestly:

- **What does this actually cost me?** Time / mood / energy. Be concrete.
- **If the situation went perfectly, what would it look like in detail?** Peterson's question — imagine your wildest realistic version.
- **Where am I bringing something to it that's making it worse?** Even 10% of "it's me" is the part you can change.
- **What's the smallest step I'd actually take this week?** Shrink it until you'd take it happily.

→ Create or update a [[Experiments/]] note for what you'll try.

## 4. Review last week's experiments (15 min)

- [ ] [[Experiments/]] — worked / partial / no — adjust:
- [ ] [[Experiments/]] — 

For each: extend, adjust, or close. Don't leave them ambiguous.

## 5. Past sort (10 min, optional)

Is there a memory or regret pulling on me this week? Don't try to solve it now. Just name it, and decide whether to spend 10 minutes on it before sleep one night this week.

- 

## 6. Vision check (5 min)

Open [[Vision]]. Read three lines. Am I drifting? In what direction?



## 7. Targeted self-reward (5 min)

One specific thing I did this week that was good. Say it precisely.
